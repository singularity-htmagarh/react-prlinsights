Digital Banking Platform

The Challenge

A leading fintech company specializing in digital banking was struggling with high customer acquisition costs and unclear understanding of which marketing activities were driving quality customers versus low-value sign-ups.

  • High customer acquisition costs across channels
  • Difficulty distinguishing quality vs. quantity in acquisitions
  • Complex customer journey spanning multiple touchpoints
  • Regulatory constraints affecting data usage

Our Solution

We applied causal inference modeling to understand the true incremental impact of marketing activities, combined with sophisticated cross-channel optimization to improve both acquisition quality and cost efficiency.

1

Incrementality Testing

Designed experiments to measure true causal impact of campaigns

2

Quality Modeling

Built models to predict customer lifetime value at acquisition

3

Cross-channel Optimization

Optimized budget allocation based on incremental value

The Results

The causal inference approach revealed significant over-attribution to certain channels and identified high-impact, low-cost opportunities that dramatically improved marketing efficiency while maintaining acquisition quality.
